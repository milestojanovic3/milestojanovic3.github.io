The InfoQ Podcast

5 essential software architecture podcasts to listen to in 2019

LISTEN ON SOUNDCLOUD

During the last few months, InfoQ has talked with some of the most well known and respected figures in Software Architecture trying to identify new trends coming up or discussing the evolution of the ones that have been gaining wider adoption. To cover topics from basic principles that are still important such as Domain Driven Design and Refactoring, to Microservices,Service Meshes, and Site Reliability Engineering we picked the brains of people like Martin Fowler, Tanya Reilly, and Vaughn Vernon in an effort to empower the community to innovate faster and build better software.

If you decide to make the investment and put aside 2 hours to go to this playlist or enjoy them separately (30 minutes each) we hope you will be inspired., and perhaps you will also share the knowledge you have acquired with your teams and accelerate the software side of progress, putting your company at the front of this development.

Things you will learn from these podcasts

  • Evolutionary architecture is a broad principle that architecture is constantly changing. While related to Microservices, it’s not Microservices by another name. You could evolve towards or away from Microservices for example.
  • The role of SRE is to define how to do things in a really reliable way. The focus is to make the majority of the operations work go away, and, for the things that can’t go away, it’s as easy as possible
  • Vlingo is an open source system for building distributed, concurrent, event-driven, reactive microservices that supports (at the framework level) Domain Driven Design.
  • The operating system of today is really unchanged since the Sun workstation of the 90’s. Yet everything else about software has really changed such as automation, build pipelines, and delivery.
  • Consul is first and foremost a centralized service registry that provides discovery. While it has a key-value store, it is Consul’s least important feature.
